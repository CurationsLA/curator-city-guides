# Santa Monica Guide

The CurationsLA Christmas and Holiday Guide to Santa Monica

A seaside winter wonderland where skating rinks, tree-lightings and holiday dining meet Pacific sunsets

Santa Monica transforms into a coastal holiday destination where the Pacific Ocean provides a stunning backdrop for festive celebrations that blend beach culture with winter traditions. Ice at Santa Monica brings outdoor ice skating to the downtown area, while the Third Street Promenade dazzles with holiday lights, street performers incorporating seasonal themes and pop-up markets featuring local artisans (santamonica.com). The Santa Monica Pier, iconic with its solar-powered Ferris wheel, hosts special holiday events including the Pacific Park tree lighting and Surfing Santa photo opportunities that epitomize Southern California's unique approach to Christmas (pacpark.com). From oceanfront hotels offering holiday packages to the Montana Avenue shopping district's festive charm, Santa Monica delivers accessible beach-town celebrations perfect for families and visitors seeking that laid-back coastal vibe (visitsantamonica.com).

## Eat

Begin your morning at Huckleberry Bakery & Café, where Zoe Nathan's naturally leavened breads, seasonal pastries and farm-to-table breakfast dishes draw devoted crowds; arrive early or expect waits during the holiday season (huckleberrycafe.com). For beachfront breakfast, Back on the Beach Café at the Santa Monica Pier serves casual fare with oceanfront seating and unbeatable views (backonthebeachcafe.com). The Misfit Restaurant + Bar on Santa Monica Boulevard offers all-day dining with comfort food classics and craft cocktails in an industrial-chic setting (themisfitbar.com).

Afternoon and evening dining options span the spectrum of coastal California cuisine. Terrazza Lounge at Hotel Casa del Mar presents an elegant ocean-view setting for holiday cocktails and small plates, with the historic hotel's Mediterranean Revival architecture creating a timeless atmosphere (hotelcasadelmar.com). For fine dining, Orla by Michael Mina at the Regent Santa Monica Beach showcases seasonal tasting menus and ocean views, with special Christmas Eve and Christmas Day services (michaelmina.net).

Rustic Canyon, hidden in a Wilshire Boulevard courtyard, serves farm-to-table California cuisine with daily changing menus reflecting what's fresh at the farmers market; the intimate space and wine program have earned it numerous accolades (rusticcanyonrestaurant.com). Mélisse, Chef Josiah Citrin's two-Michelin-star restaurant, offers refined French technique applied to California ingredients in multi-course tasting menus perfect for special celebrations (melisse.com).

For more casual options, True Food Kitchen emphasizes healthy, seasonal dishes with a menu designed in consultation with Dr. Andrew Weil (truefoodkitchen.com). Esters Wine Shop & Bar combines natural wines with California-Mediterranean small plates in a neighborhood setting (esterswineshop.com). Don't miss The Lobster for seafood with pier views, or Bay Cities Italian Deli for legendary Godmother sandwiches that have achieved cult status (thelobster.com) (baycitiesitaliandeli.com).

## Do

Ice at Santa Monica operates from Nov 7 2025 through Jan 19 2026 at Fifth Street and Arizona Avenue, bringing outdoor ice skating to downtown with daily sessions, skate rentals and a warming hut serving hot chocolate and seasonal treats (downtownsm.com). The Nov 20 2025 City Tree Lighting ceremony on the Third Street Promenade officially kicks off the holiday season with performances from local schools, caroling and the illumination of the massive community Christmas tree (santamonica.com).

Throughout December, Holiday Thursdays on the Promenade (Dec 4, 11 & 18 2025) feature extended shopping hours, street performances, special promotions and festive activities creating a European-style market atmosphere (downtownsm.com). Pacific Park at Santa Monica Pier lights a 40-foot Christmas tree in early December and hosts Surfing Santa on Dec 5 & 12 2025, where families can meet Santa in beach-appropriate attire for uniquely California photo opportunities (pacpark.com).

The Montana Avenue shopping district hosts its Holiday Walk & Marketplace on Dec 6 2025, with local boutiques staying open late, wine tastings, live music and artisan vendors creating a charming neighborhood celebration (visitsantamonica.com). Santa Monica Place, the three-level outdoor shopping center, presents holiday window displays, extended hours and seasonal promotions throughout December (santamonicaplace.com).

For outdoor activities, the Original Muscle Beach remains accessible year-round for fitness enthusiasts, while the beach bike path extends 22 miles from Pacific Palisades to Torrance, perfect for winter cycling with ocean views (santamonica.gov). The Santa Monica Pier Aquarium, beneath the carousel, offers touch tanks and marine life exhibits ideal for families seeking indoor activities (healthebay.org/smpa).

The Broad Stage at Santa Monica College presents holiday concerts, theater and dance performances in a state-of-the-art venue (thebroadstage.org). Bergamot Station Arts Center, a former trolley station now housing galleries and studios, showcases contemporary art and sometimes hosts holiday exhibitions (bergamotstation.com).

## Stay

Shutters on the Beach offers quintessential Santa Monica luxury with direct beach access, an ocean-view pool, One Pico restaurant and rooms decorated in breezy coastal style; holiday packages often include breakfast and special seasonal amenities (shuttersonthebeach.com). Hotel Casa del Mar, a Mediterranean Revival landmark built in 1926, provides elegant accommodations with the Terrazza Lounge, Catch restaurant and a prime beachfront location (hotelcasadelmar.com).

Santa Monica Proper Hotel combines contemporary design with California cool, featuring a rooftop pool with 360-degree views, Onda restaurant and rooms filled with local art and custom furnishings (properhotel.com). The Fairmont Miramar Hotel & Bungalows offers 10 acres of lush gardens with bungalows, a pool area and proximity to both the beach and downtown shopping (fairmont.com/santa-monica).

For travelers seeking more accessible price points, The Georgian Hotel on Ocean Avenue provides art deco charm and ocean views in a historic building (georgianhotel.com). Best Western Plus Gateway Hotel Santa Monica offers comfortable rooms walking distance to the pier and Third Street Promenade (bestwestern.com).

Airbnb options throughout Santa Monica's residential neighborhoods provide authentic local experiences, from beach bungalows to modern apartments (airbnb.com).

## Shop

Third Street Promenade creates a pedestrian shopping experience with national retailers including Apple, Anthropologie, Urban Outfitters and more, alongside local boutiques and street performers providing entertainment (downtownsm.com). The Winterlit Holiday Market features local artisans on select December weekends, offering handcrafted jewelry, art, clothing and gifts (downtownsm.com).

Montana Avenue between 7th and 17th streets provides a more curated shopping experience with independent boutiques including Planet Blue for California casual fashion, Just One Eye for luxury goods and beauty, and longtime local favorites (montanaave.com). ZJ Boarding House specializes in skateboard culture and street fashion (zjboardinghouse.com).

Santa Monica Place anchors the southern end of downtown with three levels of indoor-outdoor shopping including Bloomingdale's, Nordstrom, All Saints and numerous specialty stores, all with extended holiday hours (santamonicaplace.com). The Santa Monica Wednesday and Saturday Farmers Markets continue year-round, offering organic produce, flowers, prepared foods and artisan goods from certified farmers (smgov.net).

For books, Diesel, A Bookstore on Montana Avenue hosts author events and stocks curated selections including holiday titles and gift books (dieselbookstore.com). Stitch Bar & Lounge combines craft cocktails with yarn and fiber arts retail, creating a unique hybrid space (stitchbarla.com).

Main Street Santa Monica, south of Pico Boulevard, features independent shops, vintage boutiques and casual restaurants in a more neighborhood-oriented setting than the Promenade (mainstreetsm.com). Fred Segal Santa Monica offers curated fashion, beauty and lifestyle goods in a multi-brand boutique format (fredsegal.com).

## Takeaway

Santa Monica's holiday season delivers beachside charm with urban sophistication, where you can ice skate blocks from the Pacific Ocean and watch sunsets over water between shopping excursions. From the pier's solar-powered Ferris wheel lit with holiday colors to Montana Avenue's boutique windows and Huckleberry's seasonal pastries, this coastal city balances relaxation with celebration. Pack layers for temperature changes from beach to evening, bring sunscreen even in December and prepare to experience why Santa Monica remains one of Southern California's most beloved year-round destinations.
