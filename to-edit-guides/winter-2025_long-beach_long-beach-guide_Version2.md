# Long Beach Guide

The CurationsLA Christmas and Holiday Guide to Long Beach

Waterfront holiday magic where harbor lights meet California beach vibes

Long Beach celebrates the holidays with maritime traditions and beachside charm, creating a unique Southern California Christmas experience. This diverse waterfront city hosts one of the region's most spectacular boat parades, transforms the historic Queen Mary into a glowing holiday attraction and fills downtown streets with seasonal markets and entertainment (visitlongbeach.com). The mild coastal weather makes outdoor celebrations accessible, from shoreline walks to al fresco dining, while neighborhoods like Retro Row, Belmont Shore and Naples Island each contribute distinctive character to the city's festive season (longbeach.gov). With an urban downtown, classic beach communities and working waterfront all in one place, Long Beach offers versatile holiday experiences for visitors seeking both relaxation and celebration (visitlongbeach.com).

## Eat

Start your morning at The Coffee Cup, a Long Beach institution since 1974 serving hearty breakfasts and comfort food in a classic diner atmosphere that feels especially cozy during the holidays (coffeecuplongbeach.com). For a more refined brunch, head to Restauration in the East Village Arts District, where seasonal farm-to-table dishes and craft cocktails draw a stylish crowd (restaurationlb.com). Nearby, Berlin Bistro offers European café vibes with German specialties and excellent coffee (berlinbistro.net).

As the day unfolds, explore the waterfront dining scene with lunch at Parker's Lighthouse, where seafood and harbor views create a quintessentially Long Beach experience (parkerslighthouse.com). For something more casual, Roscoe's House of Chicken 'N Waffles serves soul food classics that have made this small chain legendary across Southern California (roscoeschickenandwaffles.com).

Evening dining options span the spectrum from upscale to laid-back. 555 East in the American Hotel presents contemporary American cuisine in an intimate speakeasy setting, perfect for special holiday celebrations (555east.com). L'Opera Italian Restaurant has been serving classic Italian fare since 1989, with an extensive wine list and romantic ambiance ideal for date nights (loperalongbeach.com). For waterfront views, Queensview Steakhouse aboard the Queen Mary offers fine dining with a side of maritime history (queenmary.com).

Don't miss Grounds Bakery Café for artisan bread and pastries, or explore 4th Street's Retro Row district where independent restaurants like The Attic serve creative comfort food with California flair (theatticlongbeach.com). Nick's on 2nd has been a Naples Island landmark since 1999, offering Italian-American cuisine and harbor views (nicksonthe2nd.com).

## Do

The Long Beach Christmas Boat Parade typically takes place in mid-December, when dozens of decorated vessels cruise through Alamitos Bay and Naples Canals, creating a spectacular nighttime display visible from multiple viewing locations along the shore; the event often includes fireworks and shoreline festivities (lbboatparade.com). The Queen Mary's CHILL holiday experience transforms the historic ocean liner with millions of lights, ice skating, tubing runs, holiday markets and festive dining from late November through early January (queenmary.com).

Downtown Long Beach hosts holiday markets and tree lighting ceremonies, with Pine Avenue and the Promenade area becoming pedestrian-friendly celebration zones decorated with lights and seasonal displays (downtownlongbeach.org). The Long Beach Convention Center often features holiday shows and events, while the Terrace Theater presents seasonal concerts and performances (longbeachcc.com).

The Aquarium of the Pacific decorates for the season with underwater-themed holiday displays, offering a unique way to experience festive celebrations while exploring Southern California marine life (aquariumofpacific.org). Families should visit Rainbow Harbor for waterfront strolls and visits to the shops at Shoreline Village, which hosts Santa appearances and holiday activities (shorelinevillage.com).

For outdoor activities, the Long Beach waterfront bike path extends for miles along the coast, perfect for winter cycling with ocean views (longbeach.gov). Belmont Shore's 2nd Street shopping district hosts seasonal events and offers beach access just blocks from dining and shopping (belmontshore.org). The Naples Island canals provide a picturesque setting for walking tours, especially during the boat parade season when homes are decorated with lights (visitlongbeach.com).

## Stay

The Queen Mary Hotel offers historic accommodations aboard the legendary ocean liner, where original art deco staterooms provide a unique lodging experience and direct access to the CHILL holiday attractions (queenmary.com). Hotel Maya - a DoubleTree by Hilton presents tropical-modern rooms with marina views, multiple pools and easy access to the waterfront (hotelmayalongbeach.com).

The Varden Hotel in downtown Long Beach combines boutique style with central location, featuring mid-century inspired design and proximity to Pine Avenue entertainment (thevardenhotel.com). Hyatt Centric The Pike Long Beach provides contemporary rooms with harbor views and walkability to Shoreline Village and the Aquarium of the Pacific (hyatt.com).

For beachside stays, the Hotel Current Long Beach offers modern rooms near the traffic circle and easy access to Retro Row shopping (hotelcurrent.com). Families often choose the Renaissance Long Beach Hotel for its downtown convention center location and spacious accommodations (marriott.com).

Budget-conscious travelers can find well-maintained chain hotels along Pacific Coast Highway and near the airport, all providing beach access and freeway connections (visitlongbeach.com).

## Shop

Retro Row along 4th Street between Redondo and Junipero avenues features vintage boutiques, record stores, indie fashion shops and gift stores perfect for unique holiday finds (retrorowlongbeach.com). Popular stops include Meow Vintage for curated clothing, Fingerprints Music for vinyl and CDs, and The Indie Shelf for stationery and gifts (fingerprintsmusic.com).

2nd Street in Belmont Shore offers a mile-long shopping corridor with national chains, local boutiques, surf shops and specialty stores all decorated for the holidays (belmontshore.org). The Promenade in downtown Long Beach has recently revitalized with new retailers and dining options creating an urban shopping experience (downtownlongbeach.org).

Shoreline Village provides waterfront shopping with gift shops, candy stores and souvenir vendors in a village-like setting with harbor views (shorelinevillage.com). The Queen Mary's holiday markets feature artisan vendors, seasonal gifts and nautical-themed items (queenmary.com).

For locally made goods, explore the East Village Arts District where galleries and studios sell artwork, jewelry, ceramics and crafts directly from artists (eastvillageartsdistrict.com). Long Beach Flea Market at Veterans Stadium operates on third Sundays, offering vintage finds, antiques and collectibles (longbeachantiquemarket.com).

## Takeaway

Long Beach's holiday season delivers authentic maritime charm with California accessibility, from boat parades that illuminate the harbor to the Queen Mary's transformation into a winter wonderland. The diverse neighborhoods each contribute unique character, whether exploring vintage shops on Retro Row, watching decorated boats cruise the Naples Canals or ice skating aboard a historic ocean liner. Pack layers for cool harbor breezes, comfortable shoes for waterfront walks and a sense of adventure for discovering why Long Beach offers one of Southern California's most distinctive holiday celebrations.
