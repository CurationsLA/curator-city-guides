# West Hollywood Guide

The CurationsLA Christmas and Holiday Guide to West Hollywood

Where glamour meets holiday sparkle in Los Angeles' most iconic nightlife and design district

West Hollywood embraces the festive season with the sophisticated edge that defines this independent city of 35,000 residents. Home to the legendary Sunset Strip, the renowned Design District and a vibrant LGBTQ+ community that has shaped L.A.'s cultural landscape for decades, WeHo blends high-energy nightlife with boutique shopping, contemporary art galleries and culinary innovation (visitwesthollywood.com). During the holidays, West Hollywood Park transforms into an ice-skating wonderland while the Design District hosts creative workshops and pop-up experiences that reflect the neighborhood's artistic spirit (weho.org). From Mariah Carey-themed cocktails at the Mondrian's Skybar to sustainable gift shopping at boutiques along Melrose Avenue, this neighborhood delivers holiday celebrations with style, inclusivity and that unmistakable West Hollywood glamour (visitwesthollywood.com).

## Eat

Begin your holiday mornings at Urth Caffé on Melrose Avenue, where organic coffee, açaí bowls and avocado toast fuel the neighborhood's creative class; the patio provides prime people-watching opportunities (urthcaffe.com). For a more indulgent start, the Yule Tide Mornings menu at 1 Hotel West Hollywood features gingerbread waffles, festive cocktails and seasonal dishes served in the hotel's plant-filled Juniper Lounge (1hotels.com).

Brunch in West Hollywood reaches legendary status at The Abbey Food & Bar, the iconic LGBTQ+ bar and restaurant where bottomless mimosas, creative cocktails and California cuisine draw crowds throughout the week (abbeyfoodandbar.com). Nearby, Gracias Madre serves plant-based Mexican cuisine with organic ingredients in a vibrant setting perfect for group gatherings (gracias-madre.com). Pura Vita offers Mediterranean-inspired vegetarian dishes in a modern space on Sunset Boulevard (puravitarestaurant.com).

As evening arrives, Ardor at the West Hollywood EDITION presents a four-course holiday feast showcasing Chef John Fraser's vegetable-forward cuisine in an elegant dining room with an open kitchen (editionhotels.com). For classic Italian, Cecconi's West Hollywood serves hand-made pasta, wood-fired dishes and coastal Italian specialties in a sophisticated yet welcoming atmosphere (cecconisrestaurant.com). Craig's, the celebrity hotspot on Melrose Avenue, offers American comfort food and discreet booths perfect for special celebrations (craigsla.com).

Don't miss Catch LA, the seafood-focused restaurant with a rooftop setting and coastal Mediterranean menu that draws the industry crowd (catchrestaurants.com). Connie & Ted's specializes in New England-style seafood with Maine lobster rolls, oysters and clam chowder that transport diners to the Atlantic coast (connieandteds.com). For late-night dining, Mel's Drive-In on Sunset Boulevard serves classic diner fare 24/7 in a retro setting (melsdrive-in.com).

## Do

The WeHo Winter Ice Rink operates Dec 1–21 2025 on the Great Lawn at West Hollywood Park, offering daily skating sessions with themed music nights, skate rentals and a warming area serving hot chocolate; the palm trees surrounding the rink create a distinctly Southern California holiday scene (weho.org). On Dec 13 2025, the Winter Wonderland festival transforms the park into a free community celebration with craft activities, food vendors, live performances and even manufactured snowfall for kids (weho.org).

Mariah Carey's Holiday Bar pop-up returns to the Mondrian's Skybar on Nov 13 2025 for a limited seasonal run, offering Mariah-inspired cocktails, festive décor and skyline views from one of LA's most iconic rooftop venues; reservations are essential (sbe.com/mondrian). Throughout December, the Design District hosts creative workshops including the Baobab Collection wreath customizing event on Nov 20 2025 and the DIY workshop at 1 Hotel's Juniper Lounge on Dec 15 2025, providing hands-on holiday experiences (baobabcollection.com) (1hotels.com).

The Sunset Strip's legendary music venues including The Roxy Theatre, Whisky a Go Go and The Troubadour often host special holiday concerts and performances, continuing the area's rock-and-roll heritage (theroxyonsunset.com). The Pacific Design Center, known locally as the "Blue Whale" for its distinctive architecture, showcases interior design showrooms and sometimes hosts holiday exhibitions (pacificdesigncenter.com).

West Hollywood's Art & Design Walk, typically held on the first Thursday of each month, continues through December with galleries staying open late along the Robertson Boulevard and Melrose Avenue corridors (westhollywooddesigndistrict.com). For outdoor activities, West Hollywood Park offers hiking trails, tennis courts and the recently renovated recreation center (weho.org).

The Schindler House, designed by architect Rudolph Schindler in 1922, presents exhibitions of contemporary art and architecture in the landmark modernist residence (makcenter.org). The city's expansive LGBTQ+ nightlife scene from The Abbey to Rage Nightclub to Rocco's offers diverse entertainment options throughout the holidays (visitwesthollywood.com).

## Stay

1 Hotel West Hollywood delivers eco-luxury with reclaimed wood interiors, organic mattresses, in-room record players with curated vinyl, a rooftop pool with city views and the Juniper Lounge serving seasonal cocktails; holiday packages often include sustainable amenities and festive programming (1hotels.com). The West Hollywood EDITION combines contemporary design with Ian Schrager's signature style, featuring Ardor restaurant, the Lobby Bar and a rooftop pool creating a sophisticated urban resort experience (editionhotels.com).

The Mondrian Los Angeles on Sunset Boulevard offers Philippe Starck-designed rooms, the legendary Skybar with panoramic views and a poolside scene that has defined Hollywood glamour since 1996 (sbe.com/mondrian). Sunset Tower Hotel, an art deco landmark built in 1929, provides Old Hollywood elegance with Tower Bar restaurant, a pool terrace and rooms decorated with vintage furnishings (sunsettowerhotel.com).

For boutique experiences, The London West Hollywood at Beverly Hills presents British-inspired luxury with suites featuring private balconies, a rooftop pool and proximity to both West Hollywood and Beverly Hills (thelondonwesthollywood.com). Palihouse West Hollywood offers apartment-style accommodations with full kitchens, vintage furnishings and a residential feel in a converted 1960s building (palisociety.com).

Budget-conscious travelers can find reliable chain hotels along Santa Monica Boulevard, all providing walkability to the neighborhood's dining and entertainment (visitwesthollywood.com).

## Shop

The Design District along Robertson Boulevard and surrounding streets showcases high-end home furnishings, contemporary art galleries and boutiques including HD Buttercup for furniture, Boffi for Italian kitchens and bathrooms, and numerous design showrooms (westhollywooddesigndistrict.com). During the holidays, special events like the Baobab Collection wreath workshops provide hands-on experiences with luxury home fragrance brands (baobabcollection.com).

Melrose Avenue between La Cienega and Fairfax creates a shopping corridor mixing established boutiques with emerging designers, vintage shops and streetwear labels. Reformation offers sustainable fashion in an Instagram-worthy space (reformation.com). Maxfield LA curates high-fashion brands in a gallery-like setting (maxfieldla.com). The 1 Hotel Goodthings Shop stocks sustainable products, local artisan goods and eco-conscious gifts (1hotels.com).

The Grove, while technically at the border of West Hollywood and Los Angeles, provides accessible shopping with national retailers, the original Farmers Market and elaborate holiday decorations including nightly snow shows (thegrovela.com). Book Soup on Sunset Boulevard has served as the Strip's literary hub since 1975, with author signings, rare titles and a curated selection that reflects the neighborhood's creative community (booksoup.com).

For vintage fashion, Decades stocks museum-quality designer pieces and vintage couture (decadesinc.com). Fred Segal Sunset, the iconic multi-brand boutique, offers California-casual fashion and beauty products in a bungalow-style setting (fredsegal.com). The Sunday Hollywood Farmers Market continues year-round at Selma and Ivar, offering organic produce and artisan goods (sfm.org).

## Takeaway

West Hollywood's holiday season delivers sophisticated celebration with inclusive energy, where ice skating under palm trees feels as natural as sipping Mariah-inspired cocktails at a rooftop bar. From the Design District's creative workshops to the Sunset Strip's legendary nightlife and sustainable shopping along Melrose Avenue, this independent city balances glamour with community values. Pack stylish attire for upscale venues, comfortable shoes for exploring walkable streets and an open mind for experiencing why West Hollywood remains one of Los Angeles' most dynamic neighborhoods year-round.
