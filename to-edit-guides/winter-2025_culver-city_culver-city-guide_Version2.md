# Culver City Guide

The CurationsLA Christmas and Holiday Guide to Culver City

Creative energy meets holiday charm in L.A.'s arts and entertainment hub

Culver City transforms into a pedestrian-friendly holiday destination where art galleries, indie boutiques and farm-to-table restaurants create a sophisticated yet accessible celebration. Once known as the "Heart of Screenland" for its historic film studios, this walkable downtown now hosts Sony Pictures Studios while nurturing a thriving arts scene centered around the Culver City Arts District and Platform shopping complex (visitculvercity.com). During the holidays, the tree-lined streets sparkle with lights, the Culver Steps become a gathering place for community events and local businesses showcase seasonal displays that reflect the neighborhood's creative spirit (culvercityguide.com). With award-winning restaurants, contemporary art spaces and a genuine small-town vibe despite its L.A. County location, Culver City offers a refined holiday experience perfect for food lovers and culture seekers (visitculvercity.com).

## Eat

Begin your day at Gjusta Goods, the market and café offshoot of Venice's Gjusta bakery, where fresh pastries, house-roasted coffee and artisanal provisions make for a perfect breakfast or lunch stop on Washington Boulevard (gjusta.com). For brunch, Akasha serves California cuisine with organic ingredients in a bright, modern space that feels both sophisticated and welcoming; their seasonal menus often feature holiday-inspired dishes (akasharestaurant.com). Nearby, The Wallace is a neighborhood gem offering creative American fare with an emphasis on fresh, local ingredients (thewallaceculvercity.com).

As evening approaches, Vespertine provides one of Los Angeles' most avant-garde dining experiences, where Chef Jordan Kahn presents multi-course tasting menus in an otherworldly architectural setting; reservations are essential and the experience is legendary (vespertine.la). For something more casual yet equally excellent, head to Destroyer, a daytime café from Jordan Kahn serving inventive breakfast and lunch dishes in a minimalist space (destroyer.la).

Margot on Platform offers Peter Cote's Roman-inspired Italian cuisine in an elegant setting perfect for holiday celebrations, with handmade pastas and seasonal ingredients taking center stage (margotla.com). Wildcraft Culver City presents a seasonal menu emphasizing vegetables and sustainable proteins, with craft cocktails and a warm atmosphere ideal for intimate gatherings (wildcraftculvercity.com). Don't miss Sidecar Doughnuts & Coffee for artisanal doughnuts and espresso drinks that make excellent treats while holiday shopping (sidecardoughnuts.com).

For international flavors, explore Jyan Isaac Bread for Korean-inspired baked goods, or Honey's Kettle Fried Chicken for Southern comfort food that has garnered a devoted following (honeyskettlefc.com). The Platform shopping complex hosts several excellent dining options including Margot and Fiona, creating a European-style marketplace atmosphere perfect for holiday browsing and dining (theplatformla.com).

## Do

The Culver City Tree Lighting typically takes place in early December at City Hall, featuring live performances, holiday music and the illumination of the community Christmas tree, marking the official start of the season (visitculvercity.com). Throughout December, the Culver City Arts District hosts gallery walks and holiday exhibitions, with contemporary art spaces like Thinkspace Projects, Coagula Curatorial and others presenting seasonal shows (culvercityartsdistrict.com).

Platform, the open-air shopping and dining destination on National Boulevard, decorates with festive displays and often hosts pop-up markets featuring local makers, artisans and designers (theplatformla.com). The Culver Steps, a public plaza and performance space downtown, becomes a gathering spot for community celebrations, seasonal concerts and family-friendly activities (visitculvercity.com).

The Museum of Jurassic Technology offers a unique cultural experience year-round, presenting enigmatic exhibitions that blend fact and fiction in ways that captivate visitors; this one-of-a-kind institution makes for an unforgettable afternoon during the holidays (mjt.org). Sony Pictures Studio Tours allow visitors to explore the working lot where classic films and contemporary television shows are produced; advance booking is recommended (sonypicturesstudiostours.com).

For families, Baldwin Hills Scenic Overlook provides hiking trails with panoramic views of Los Angeles, perfect for crisp winter mornings before exploring downtown Culver City (parks.ca.gov). Star Eco Station, an environmental science and wildlife rescue center, offers educational programs and encounters with rescued exotic animals (ecostation.org).

## Stay

The Culver Hotel, a historic 1924 landmark building at the intersection of Culver and Washington boulevards, offers boutique accommodations in the heart of downtown with an on-site restaurant and bar; the building housed Munchkin actors during the filming of "The Wizard of Oz" (culverhotel.com). Four Points by Sheraton Los Angeles Westside provides modern rooms, a pool and easy access to both Culver City and nearby attractions like Venice Beach and Marina del Rey (marriott.com).

DoubleTree by Hilton Los Angeles Westside features comfortable accommodations with meeting space, dining and proximity to Sony Pictures Studios, making it popular with business travelers and families (hilton.com). For extended stays, Homewood Suites by Hilton offers suite-style rooms with kitchenettes, breakfast included and a residential feel convenient for holiday family gatherings (hilton.com).

Budget-conscious travelers can find well-maintained chain hotels along Sepulveda Boulevard, all providing easy freeway access to explore greater Los Angeles while being based in walkable Culver City (visitculvercity.com).

## Shop

Platform creates a curated retail experience with independent boutiques like Jenni Kayne for California-casual clothing and home goods, Clare V. for leather accessories and Poketo for design-forward gifts and stationery (theplatformla.com). Downtown Culver City along Washington and Main streets features independent shops including Mohawk General Store for contemporary design items, Sundays for beachwear and lifestyle goods, and h.d. buttercup for furniture and home décor (visitculvercity.com).

The Culver City Arts District galleries often sell artwork directly from exhibitions, providing unique holiday gifts for art collectors (culvercityartsdistrict.com). Gelsons Market on Jefferson Boulevard offers gourmet groceries, prepared foods and gift baskets perfect for hostess presents or stocking stuffers (gelsons.com).

Culver City Farmers Market operates year-round on Tuesdays at Main Street, featuring certified farmers, artisan food vendors and local craftspeople selling seasonal goods (culvercityfarmersmarket.com). For books, Pages a Bookstore stocks curated titles and hosts author events, serving as a community literary hub (pagesabookstore.com).

The holiday season brings pop-up markets to various locations around downtown Culver City, where local designers, jewelers, ceramicists and makers showcase handcrafted items perfect for gift-giving (visitculvercity.com).

## Takeaway

Culver City's holiday season offers a sophisticated urban village experience where walkable streets connect award-winning restaurants, contemporary galleries and indie boutiques. From the architectural marvel of Vespertine to the community warmth of the tree lighting ceremony, this neighborhood balances cutting-edge culture with genuine hospitality. Pack comfortable walking shoes for exploring the compact downtown, bring a camera for Platform's Instagram-worthy corners and prepare to discover why Culver City has become one of L.A.'s most compelling holiday destinations.
