# Burbank Guide

The CurationsLA Christmas and Holiday Guide to Burbank

Studio-town cheer meets family traditions in L.A.'s entertainment capital

Burbank embraces the holidays with the warmth of a small town and the star power of a studio capital. Home to Warner Bros., Disney Studios and other entertainment giants, this city of 100,000 offers a unique holiday experience where soundstages and backlots become festive backdrops for community celebrations (visitburbank.com). Downtown Burbank and Magnolia Park districts transform with twinkling lights, seasonal window displays and locally owned shops that invite leisurely browsing (visitburbank.com). The mild winter weather makes outdoor events accessible, from tree lightings to holiday parades, while family-friendly dining and entertainment options cater to visitors of all ages seeking that classic California Christmas vibe (myburbankholidays.com).

## Eat

Start your holiday mornings at Romancing the Bean, a charming neighborhood café serving artisan coffee and homemade pastries in a cozy atmosphere perfect for planning your day's adventures (romancingthebean.com). For brunch, Granville on San Fernando Boulevard offers American comfort food with seasonal twists, featuring spacious booths and a welcoming vibe that works for family gatherings or casual meetups (granvillecafe.com). Nearby, Coral Café has been a Burbank institution since 1959, known for hearty breakfasts, fresh-baked pies and that retro diner charm that evokes holiday nostalgia (coralcafe.com).

As evening approaches, Porto's Bakery & Café draws crowds for Cuban pastries, empanadas, cakes and holiday treats that have become legendary across Los Angeles; the Burbank location offers ample space and festive displays during December (portosbakery.com). For dinner, head to Tallyrand Restaurant, established in 1960 and serving classic American steakhouse fare in an Old Hollywood setting complete with red leather booths and vintage décor (tallyrandrestaurant.com). Castaway Burbank perched in the Verdugo Mountains provides panoramic views of the San Fernando Valley alongside contemporary American cuisine, making it ideal for special holiday celebrations and sunset cocktails (castawayburbank.com).

Don't miss Shake Shack Burbank for casual bites, or explore the diverse options at the Burbank Town Center food court for international flavors (visitburbank.com). Bob's Big Boy, the oldest remaining location of this classic chain, serves up nostalgia with burgers and milkshakes in its iconic googie architecture (bigboy.com).

## Do

The Burbank Holiday Street Fair typically transforms downtown into a festive marketplace in early December, with local vendors, food trucks, live music and family activities creating a community celebration (visitburbank.com). Burbank's annual Tree Lighting Ceremony at Downtown Burbank brings the community together for caroling, performances and the illumination of the city's official holiday tree (myburbankholidays.com).

Warner Bros. Studio Tour Hollywood offers holiday-themed experiences during December, allowing visitors to explore working soundstages, backlots and prop departments while seeing how the studio decorates for seasonal productions; advance booking is essential as these tours sell out quickly (wbstudiotour.com). Nearby, the Burbank Town Center mall hosts Santa visits and holiday photo opportunities throughout December, with extended shopping hours and seasonal promotions (burbanktowncenter.com).

Families should explore Stough Canyon Nature Center in the Verdugo Mountains, where winter hiking trails offer crisp air and views of the valley below; the center often hosts seasonal nature programs and guided walks (ci.burbank.ca.us). The historic Starlight Bowl in Burbank sometimes features holiday concerts, taking advantage of California's mild winter evenings for outdoor performances (starlightbowl.com).

For a unique experience, catch a show at Flappers Comedy Club, which often includes holiday-themed comedy nights perfect for adults seeking entertainment beyond family-friendly festivities (flapperscomedy.com). The Colony Theatre presents local productions and sometimes includes holiday plays or musical performances (colonytheatre.org).

## Stay

The Hotel Amarano Burbank offers boutique accommodations in a Spanish Colonial Revival building, with complimentary breakfast, evening wine receptions and a pool area that feels like an urban oasis; its location near the studios makes it popular with entertainment industry visitors during pilot season and holidays (hotelamarano.com). Coast Anabelle Hotel provides comfortable rooms with modern amenities, a restaurant and bar, and easy access to both downtown Burbank and nearby Universal Studios (coastanabellehotel.com).

For extended stays, the Residence Inn by Marriott Burbank Downtown features suites with full kitchens, ideal for families or groups celebrating the holidays together while having space to spread out (marriott.com). The Safari Inn, a retro motor lodge with mid-century charm, has appeared in numerous films and TV shows, offering a kitschy yet comfortable base for exploring Burbank's attractions (safariinnburbank.com).

Budget-conscious travelers can choose from several well-maintained chain hotels along Olive Avenue, all providing easy freeway access and proximity to studios and downtown entertainment (visitburbank.com).

## Shop

Magnolia Park, Burbank's vintage shopping district, spans several blocks along Magnolia Boulevard with antique stores, vintage clothing boutiques, record shops and quirky gift stores perfect for finding unique holiday presents (magnoliapark.com). Popular stops include Blast From The Past for pop culture memorabilia, It's A Wrap! for wardrobe pieces from film and TV productions, and Magnolia Park Vintage for curated mid-century finds (magnoliapark.com).

Downtown Burbank anchors the city's retail scene with the Burbank Town Center mall offering national chains, dining and holiday events under one roof (burbanktowncenter.com). San Fernando Boulevard features a walkable stretch of local boutiques, gift shops and the historic Golden Mall area.

For book lovers, Dark Delicacies stocks horror, mystery and sci-fi titles alongside author events and signings that continue through December (darkdel.com). Porto's Bakery becomes a holiday destination in itself, with special seasonal pastries, cakes and gift boxes that make excellent presents for food-loving friends and family (portosbakery.com).

The Burbank Farmers Market operates year-round on Saturdays along Olive Avenue, offering locally grown produce, artisan goods, food vendors and live entertainment even during winter months (ci.burbank.ca.us).

## Takeaway

Burbank's holiday season offers an authentic Southern California experience where studio glamour meets small-town traditions. From vintage shopping in Magnolia Park to studio tours and downtown festivities, this entertainment hub provides accessible celebrations perfect for families and visitors seeking that classic California Christmas. Pack comfortable walking shoes for exploring districts on foot, bring layers for cool evenings and embrace the behind-the-scenes magic that makes Burbank a legendary holiday destination.
