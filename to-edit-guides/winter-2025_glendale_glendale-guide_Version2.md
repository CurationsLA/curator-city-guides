# Glendale Guide

The CurationsLA Christmas and Holiday Guide to Glendale

Family traditions meet modern shopping in this diverse San Fernando Valley city

Glendale transforms into a family-friendly holiday destination where the Americana at Brand shopping complex creates a European village atmosphere complete with trolley rides, dancing fountains and elaborate decorations. This city of over 200,000 residents celebrates its diverse cultural heritage during the holidays, with Armenian, Latino and Asian communities contributing unique traditions to the seasonal festivities (visitglendaleca.com). The walkable downtown Brand Boulevard corridor connects the Americana to Glendale Galleria, creating an extensive retail district that becomes a shopper's paradise during December (visitglendaleca.com). From classic tree lighting ceremonies to contemporary dining experiences, Glendale offers accessible holiday celebrations perfect for multi-generational gatherings and visitors seeking that warm community vibe (glendaleca.gov).

## Eat

Start your morning at Porto's Bakery & Café, the beloved Cuban bakery that originated in Glendale and draws lines around the block for potato balls, guava cheese rolls, tres leches cakes and strong Cuban coffee; the festive atmosphere during holidays makes the wait worthwhile (portosbakery.com). For a sit-down breakfast, head to Zankou Chicken, the legendary Armenian rotisserie chicken restaurant where garlic sauce and hummus have achieved cult status across Los Angeles (zankouchicken.com).

Brunch seekers should explore Damon's Steakhouse, a mid-century classic that has been serving prime cuts and old-school cocktails since 1937, complete with red leather booths and that vintage supper club atmosphere (damonssteakhouse.com). Nearby, The Original Fish Company on Brand Boulevard offers seafood in a casual setting perfect for family lunches (theoriginalfishcompany.com).

As evening arrives, experience fine dining at Katsuya Glendale where celebrity chef Katsuya Uechi presents modern Japanese cuisine and innovative sushi in a sleek setting (sbe.com/katsuya). For Italian, Divino serves rustic dishes and an extensive wine list in an intimate space (divinopizzeria.com). Carousel Restaurant provides authentic Armenian mezze, kebabs and hospitality that has made it a community institution for over three decades (carouselrestaurant.com).

Don't miss Raffi's Place for more Armenian specialties, or explore the diverse dining options at the Americana where Din Tai Fung serves legendary soup dumplings and Ketchup offers creative American comfort food (americanaatbrand.com). For casual bites, In-N-Out Burger's original location isn't far, or grab late-night shawarma at one of the many Middle Eastern restaurants along Central Avenue (in-n-out.com).

## Do

The Americana at Brand becomes a holiday spectacle from late November through January, with a 100-foot Christmas tree, nightly snow shows at 7pm, dancing fountain performances synchronized to seasonal music, trolley rides for kids and elaborate decorations throughout the outdoor shopping complex (americanaatbrand.com). The Glendale Galleria next door provides indoor shopping with extended holiday hours and Santa photo opportunities (glendalegalleria.com).

Glendale's official Tree Lighting Ceremony typically takes place in early December at Brand Boulevard, bringing the community together for caroling, performances from local schools and the illumination of the city tree (glendaleca.gov). The Alex Theatre, a historic art deco landmark on Brand Boulevard, presents holiday concerts, comedy shows and classic film screenings throughout December in a beautifully restored 1925 venue (alextheatre.org).

Brand Park, nestled in the Verdugo Mountains, offers hiking trails with views of Glendale and the San Fernando Valley, plus the Brand Library & Art Center which hosts exhibitions in the historic Miradero mansion (glendaleca.gov). The Autry Museum of the American West in nearby Griffith Park presents holiday programs exploring how different cultures celebrated in the frontier era (theautry.org).

Families should visit the Museum of Neon Art (MONA) to see electrifying displays of vintage signs and contemporary light art (neonmona.org). For outdoor activities, Verdugo Park Recreation Center offers sports facilities and community programs, while Deukmejian Wilderness Park provides more rugged hiking in the surrounding hills (glendaleca.gov).

## Stay

The Hilton Los Angeles North/Glendale & Executive Meeting Center provides full-service accommodations with meeting space, dining and proximity to both the Americana and Glendale Galleria, making it popular with business travelers and families (hilton.com). The Glendale Civic Suites by Hyatt House offers extended-stay accommodations with kitchenettes and a residential feel in downtown Glendale (hyatt.com).

For boutique experiences, the Hotel Indigo Los Angeles Downtown presents stylish rooms with local design touches, though technically in downtown LA it's easily accessible to Glendale (ihg.com). Courtyard by Marriott Los Angeles Pasadena/Old Town sits at the border between Glendale and Pasadena, offering modern amenities and walkability to Old Town Pasadena (marriott.com).

Budget travelers can find reliable chain hotels along Colorado Street and near the 134 freeway, all providing easy access to Glendale's shopping districts and the greater Los Angeles area (visitglendaleca.com).

## Shop

The Americana at Brand anchors Glendale's retail scene with over 75 stores including Apple, Anthropologie, Tiffany & Co., Ted Baker and numerous others in an open-air setting designed to evoke European shopping villages (americanaatbrand.com). During the holidays, pop-up markets featuring local artisans and seasonal gift vendors appear throughout the complex.

Glendale Galleria provides indoor shopping with department stores like Macy's, Target and over 200 specialty retailers, plus extended holiday hours and gift-wrapping services (glendalegalleria.com). Brand Boulevard between the two shopping centers features additional boutiques, gift shops and local businesses.

For books, Barnes & Noble at the Americana stocks holiday titles and hosts author events, while Abril Bookstore specializes in Spanish language books and gifts (barnesandnoble.com). Armenag Bookstore on San Fernando Road offers Armenian language books, music and cultural items (armenagbookstore.com).

Antique lovers should explore the vintage shops along San Fernando Road and Brand Boulevard where mid-century furniture, collectibles and unique holiday décor can be discovered. The Glendale Farmers Market operates on Thursdays year-round at the Glendale Civic Auditorium, offering local produce, artisan goods and prepared foods (glendaleca.gov).

## Takeaway

Glendale's holiday season delivers family-friendly celebrations in a city that balances suburban comfort with urban amenities. From the Americana's nightly snow shows to Porto's legendary pastries and the diverse culinary scene reflecting the community's multicultural character, this San Fernando Valley destination offers accessible holiday magic. Pack comfortable walking shoes for shopping marathons, bring an appetite for Armenian cuisine and prepare to experience why Glendale has become a beloved holiday destination for locals and visitors alike.
