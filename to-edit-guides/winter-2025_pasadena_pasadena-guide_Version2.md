# Pasadena Guide

The CurationsLA Christmas and Holiday Guide to Pasadena

Historic charm, parade traditions and elegant holiday displays in the Crown City

Pasadena transforms into a Victorian holiday wonderland each winter, where century-old traditions meet contemporary cultural celebrations. Known for hosting the Tournament of Roses Parade on New Year's Day, this historic city begins its festivities weeks earlier with elaborate tree lightings, world-class museum exhibitions and dining experiences set against a backdrop of Craftsman architecture and California sunshine (cityofpasadena.net). The charming Old Town district becomes a pedestrian-friendly celebration hub with twinkling lights strung between buildings, while the nearby Arroyo Seco and Rose Bowl create additional gathering spaces for community festivities (pasadenanow.com). From elegant afternoon teas to family-friendly parades, Pasadena offers a refined yet accessible holiday experience steeped in Southern California tradition (visitpasadena.com).

## Eat

Start your holiday morning at Urth Caffé in Old Town Pasadena, where organic coffee and seasonal pastries fuel shoppers exploring Colorado Boulevard (urthcaffe.com). For a classic brunch experience, head to The Raymond 1886, a historic Craftsman bungalow serving elevated American cuisine amid holiday decorations and cozy fireplaces (theraymond.com). Nearby, Pie 'n Burger has been a Pasadena institution since 1963, offering comfort food and homemade pies perfect for a casual lunch between errands (pienburger.com).

As evening approaches, Union Pasadena presents an upscale Italian menu in an intimate setting, with seasonal dishes highlighting winter produce and housemade pastas (unionpasadena.com). For a truly special occasion, book a table at The Langham Huntington's The Royce, where Chef David Féau crafts contemporary American cuisine with French technique; the restaurant often features prix-fixe holiday menus and New Year's Eve celebrations (langhamhotels.com). Don't miss afternoon tea at The Langham Huntington's Lobby Lounge, a beloved tradition featuring three-tiered presentations of finger sandwiches, scones with Devonshire cream and seasonal pastries served in an elegant setting (langhamhotels.com). For something more casual yet festive, Bone Kettle in South Pasadena serves craft ramen and small plates in a neighborhood setting that feels both cozy and contemporary (bonekettle.com).

## Do

The Pasadena Tree Lighting ceremony typically takes place in late November at Pasadena City Hall, marking the official start of the holiday season with live performances, caroling and the illumination of a towering tree adorned with thousands of lights (cityofpasadena.net). Throughout December, Old Town Pasadena hosts free concerts and holiday markets, with local artisans setting up along Colorado Boulevard and One Colorado courtyard (oldpasadena.org).

The Norton Simon Museum offers a serene holiday experience with world-class European and Asian art collections displayed in sculpture gardens decorated with seasonal touches; the museum often extends hours during December and hosts special evening programs (nortonsimon.org). Nearby, The Huntington Library, Art Museum and Botanical Gardens presents a breathtaking holiday display, with the historic mansion decked in period decorations and the grounds featuring themed gardens perfect for winter strolls (huntington.org). Descanso Gardens in La Cañada Flintridge, just north of Pasadena, hosts Enchanted Forest of Light from November through January, where visitors walk through illuminated forest paths with interactive art installations and projected light displays (descansogardens.org).

For families, Kidspace Children's Museum in Brookside Park offers holiday-themed programming, including craft workshops and seasonal storytimes (kidspacemuseum.org). The Rose Bowl Stadium area often hosts holiday runs and community events leading up to the New Year, creating opportunities for active celebrations under California's mild winter skies (rosebowlstadium.com). As December turns to January, excitement builds for the Tournament of Roses Parade on Jan 1 2026, when elaborate floral floats, marching bands and equestrian units process down Colorado Boulevard in one of America's most iconic New Year's Day traditions (tournamentofroses.com).

## Stay

The Langham Huntington, Pasadena sits on 23 acres at the base of the San Gabriel Mountains, offering a historic resort experience with multiple dining options, a spa and holiday packages that include breakfast and special amenities (langhamhotels.com). For a boutique experience, The Bissell House Bed & Breakfast provides Victorian elegance in a carefully restored 1887 mansion, where guests enjoy gourmet breakfasts and personalized service just blocks from Old Town (bissellhouse.com).

Families often choose the Westin Pasadena for its central location near the Convention Center and Old Town shopping district, with spacious rooms and easy access to holiday events (marriott.com). The historic Pasadena Playhouse District also features The Saga Motor Hotel, a renovated mid-century property with modern design and walkability to dining and entertainment (sagapasadena.com). For extended stays, Sheraton Pasadena offers suites and business amenities convenient to both downtown Pasadena and nearby Arcadia attractions (marriott.com).

## Shop

Old Town Pasadena transforms into a shopper's paradise during the holidays, with Colorado Boulevard hosting over 200 retailers from national brands to independent boutiques (oldpasadena.org). Woman's Bookstore, one of the oldest feminist bookstores in the nation, offers curated titles and hosts author events throughout December (womansbookstore.com). Nearby, Vroman's Bookstore anchors the district with three floors of books, gifts and a café, making it a popular destination for holiday browsing and gift-wrapping services (vromansbookstore.com).

For unique finds, explore the artisan vendors at Jackalope Fair, which sometimes pops up in Pasadena during December, featuring handcrafted goods from hundreds of makers (jackalopefair.com). The Pasadena Antique Mall provides vintage treasures and collectibles for those seeking one-of-a-kind gifts (pasadenaantiquedesigncenter.com). South Lake Avenue offers another shopping corridor with national retailers and local boutiques, all decorated for the season (southlakedistrict.com).

The Rose Bowl Flea Market, held on the second Sunday of each month, continues through winter with vendors selling vintage clothing, furniture, jewelry and holiday décor; the December edition is particularly festive (rgcshows.com). Paseo Colorado, an open-air shopping center in the heart of Pasadena, hosts holiday events and provides covered walkways connecting specialty shops and restaurants (paseocoloradopasadena.com). For food gifts, Nicole's Gourmet Foods offers artisanal provisions, prepared meals and holiday catering that showcase local California ingredients (nicolesgourmetfoods.com).

## Takeaway

Pasadena's holiday season blends historic elegance with contemporary California style, offering visitors a refined yet relaxed celebration. From world-class museums and botanical gardens to Victorian architecture and New Year's parade traditions, this Crown City provides cultural depth alongside festive cheer. Pack layers for cool evenings, comfortable shoes for exploring Old Town and a camera to capture the Tournament of Roses preparations that make Pasadena a legendary Southern California destination each winter.
